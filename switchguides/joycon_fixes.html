Joy-Con Pairing Fixes

Welcome to the Joy-Con pairing fixes guide. This guide is structured into three levels, each designed to address increasingly complex pairing issues. Start with Level 1 and progress to higher levels if the issue persists.

Level 1: Basic Joy-Con Resync

This level resolves most common issues where the Joy-Con does not sync properly. Follow these steps:

  1. In jailbreak mode, attach the Joy-Cons to the Switch in handheld orientation.
  2. Go to Settings.
  3. Select Controllers and Sensors.
  4. Scroll down and choose Disconnect Controller (hold X).
  5. Remove the Joy-Cons.
  6. Reconnect them.
  7. Remove the Joy-Cons and disconnect them from the settings.
  8. Open legit mode and repeat the steps above.

This procedure should resolve most pairing issues. If it doesn't, proceed to Level 2.

Level 2: Advanced Pairing with Data Sync from OFW

This level involves more in-depth steps and syncing pairing data from the official firmware (OFW). Follow these steps:

  1. Ensure the Nintendo Switch is in official firmware (OFW) or legit mode with Joy-Cons attached.
  2. Access the settings screen and choose Disconnect Controllers.
  3. Remove the Joy-Cons and press and hold the sync button until the SL/SR lights turn off.
  4. Disable Bluetooth or activate airplane mode on the Switch.
  5. Power off the Switch.
  6. Power on the Switch in legit mode.
  7. Attach the Joy-Cons.
  8. Enable Bluetooth and disable airplane mode.
  9. Repeat steps 3, 4, 5, and 6.
  10. Power off the Switch.
  11. Access Hekate.
  12. Open payloads.
  13. Launch Tegraexplorer.
  14. Run the script syncfix.te (Note: This will trigger a red initialization screen, but your data will remain intact).
  15. Wait for the script to finish, then power off the Switch.
  16. Open official firmware (OFW) and observe if the issue is resolved.

Please note that these steps involve accessing custom firmware and require caution. Ensure you understand the risks before proceeding.
